Summary

This project utilizes Python language, Open Weather API, pandas data frame and JSON format for storing and transporting datatraversals to try and answer fundamental question: “What’s the weather like as we move from North & South poles towards the equator?”

We chose 1,500 random Latitude and Longitudes and utilized the Python CitiPy library to find the cities closest to the chosen Latitude & Longitude (while dropping the duplicates) to get a listing of over 550 cities. We utilize OpenWeatherMapAPI to obtain the weather for the chosen cities while dropping cities with no data to create a dataframe of weather information for the cities.

The dataframe is saved to CSV and analyzed across three climate zones (Tropical, Temperate, and Polar). To showcase the relationship between latitude and maximum temperature/humidity/cloudness/wind speed, we utilize MatplotLib scatter plots for the below visuals:

  • Temperature (F) vs. Latitude
  • Humidity (%) vs. Latitude
  • Cloudness (%) vs. Latitude
  • Wind Speed (mph) vs. Latitude
